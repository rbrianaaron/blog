The sum of absolute deviations from the mean and median

Date Sun 30 November 2014 By Brian Aaron Category Reflections. Tags Python mean median

While studying the concepts of the sum of the absolute deviations from the mean and median, I noted something peculiar.

The commonly taught concept is that the median rather than the mean produces the smallest sum of absolute deviations and thus the smallest mean of absolute deviations.

Just to review, for the following data: 1, 3, 3, 4, 5. The median, or middle number, is 3. The arithmetic mean is 3.2.

The equation to find the sum of the absolute deviation from the median is:

|1-3| + |3-3| + |3-3| + |4-3| + |5-3| = 5

Using a similar equation, the sum of the absolute deviations from the mean is 5.2. The median produces the smaller number.
